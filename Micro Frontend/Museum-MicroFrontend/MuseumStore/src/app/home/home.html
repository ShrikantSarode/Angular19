Vermont Mesum of Art

About the VMoA

The VMOA’s free permanent collection spans more than 5,000 years, from ancient Egypt to the present, and its 164-acre Museum Park showcases the connection between art and nature through site-specific works of environmental art.

History of VMoA

VMoA, launched in 2020 by artist Stuart Semple, is the world’s first fully virtual art museum. Conceived in 1999 but realized during the COVID-19 pandemic, it offers a free, immersive experience with curated global artworks. Designed by Emily Mann and curated by Lee Cavaliere, VMoA aims to democratize art access and challenge elitism in the art world. The Virtual Museum of Art (VMoA), founded by British artist Stuart Semple, is the world’s first fully virtual art museum. Conceived in 1999 and launched in September 2020, VMoA was born out of Semple’s long-standing vision to democratize access to art and challenge the exclusivity of traditional institutions. The COVID-19 pandemic provided the perfect moment to realize this idea, as global lockdowns pushed cultural experiences online1. VMoA is not just a website—it’s a fully immersive, downloadable museum experience. Designed by Emily Mann, the virtual architecture mimics the spatial dynamics of a real museum, while the curatorial direction was led by Lee Cavaliere, a respected art dealer and curator2. The museum features digital surrogates of physical artworks, many on loan from major institutions, alongside contemporary pieces from emerging artists. This blend of classic and modern art fosters a rich, inclusive environment for global audiences. Unlike traditional museums, VMoA has no physical building or permanent collection.
